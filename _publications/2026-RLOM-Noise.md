The effect of noise on speculative trading remains unclear. We introduce reinforcement learning to examine the effect in limit order markets. We show that intensive noise liquidity provision increases uninformed chasing, improves market liquidity and price efficiency, while intensive noise liquidity consumption increases informed aggressive liquidity provision and improves price efficiency, but reduces market liquidity. Consistent with U-shaped price efficiency to the noise trading uncertainty on liquidity provision and consumption, we find that hump-shaped (U-shaped) order profitabilities for informed (uninformed) are associated with U-shaped noise trading costs. Therefore, at increasing costs, intensive noise liquidity provision improves market liquidity, price efficiency, order profitabilities of informed traders, and reduces the loss, even makes profit, for uninformed traders.
