Under the macroeconomic background of accelerating the return of the financial system to serving the real economy, improving the information efficiency of the capital market by optimizing the investor structure to achieve a precise allocation of real economic resources has become a key issue in the deepening reform of China's capital market. However, due to the difficulty of isolating the interference of discount rate fluctuations using traditional empirical frameworks, there remains no academic consensus on the core question of whether the institutionalization of individual investors truly enhances market information efficiency. Using data from China's A-share market and mutual funds from 2005 to 2024, this paper systematically examines the governance effect of mutual fund holdings on market information efficiency. The findings reveal the following. First, mutual fund holdings significantly improve market information efficiency, and this conclusion remains robust after addressing endogeneity issues using the CSI 300 Index adjustment as an instrumental variable. Second, the mechanism analysis shows that mutual fund holdings increase the amount of firm-external environmental information embedded in stock prices, thereby significantly enhancing the sensitivity of corporate investment and financing decisions to stock price signals, which translates information efficiency into the efficiency of real resource allocation. Third, this governance effect is significantly heterogeneous across fund types, driven primarily by actively managed funds with active information-gathering capabilities, whereas the role of passively managed funds is relatively limited.
