This paper investigates how heterogeneous trading behavior of retail investors, identified through account-level data and a stockholding-based clustering approach, translates into market impact. Prevalent "retail-as-noise" view masks crucial systematic differences that distinctly shape market quality. Specifically, Premium Speculators, who prefer high-price-and-attention and shell-value stocks, are associated with lower future returns, consistent with overreaction to short-term price movements and underreaction to fundamentals. In contrast, Buzz Investors, who favor high-price-and-attention and value stocks, show positive return predictability, which is more consistent with superior public-information processing. Our results highlight how retail investor heterogeneity shapes market dynamics through distinct trading behaviors and price discovery.
