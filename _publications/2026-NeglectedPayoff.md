We study how heterogeneous awareness of a newly salient payoff component affects price formation, liquidity, and return predictability in a noisy rational-expectations equilibrium. A fraction of traders neglects the newly salient component and therefore fails to recognize that other traders are informed about, and trade on, this component. These misspecifications make equilibrium prices partially informative about the component while inducing unaware traders to under-infer information from prices. As a result, unaware traders provide liquidity by trading aggressively against price movements, dampening the overreaction induced by noise trading, while prices can underreact to news about the component. When awareness is intermediate, underreaction dominates and generates return momentum. We extend the model to (i) an overlapping-generations setting with increasing awareness, which can generate reversals in early generations and momentum in later generations, and (ii) a two-asset setting in which heterogeneous exposure and hedging transmit underreaction across assets, generating cross-sectional momentum.
